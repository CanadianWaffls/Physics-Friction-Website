How do Cars Work?

To understand how tires use friction to make vehicles move, we must understand the basics of how cars move. Car engines use a piston to apply force to a crankshaft which turns the axles of the car. The axel spins the wheels and by extension the tires which then push against the ground to move the car forward (Karroll & Karroll, 2024).

Where do Tires Come in?

Newton's third law states that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction (Newton's third law of Motion) Because of this, when the tires push against the ground, the ground pushes against the tire with the same amount of force. Newton's second law states that the net force of an object is proportional to the acceleration and inversely proportional to the mass (Elert). Because the ground has more mass than a car the net force of the ground is miniscule compared to that of the car so the car will move. Because the ground is pushing in the opposite direction of the tires, that is where the direction of the net force of the car will be.

How are tires affected by friction?

Friction is a force that acts on surfaces that are sliding or rolling against each other (Friction 2024). When a car's tires push against the ground it is the force of friction between the ground and the tires that push the car forward. Every surface has friction however tires are designed to amplify this friction so that the car can more easily move itself forward. This added friction is also what allows cars to brake so easily compared to other vehicles with wheels. Since cars have so much friction when there is no longer a force applied friction becomes the only force acting in the x direction (horizontal) and so the car uses up this remaining frictional force until it comes to a stop.

With Friction the tire turns and pushes off the ground, moving the car forward.

Amplifying Friction

Because friction is such a crucial factor of a good tire, tire companies must consider 3 main factors when making tires.

Tread Pattern

Tread patterns on tires are important because the irregular shape of the grooves create more points of contact with the ground that generates more friction (Karroll, 2024).

Tire Composition

The materials that are used in a tire can impact its friction. Most tires use a soft rubber because it conforms to the rough surface of the road and creates more points of contact, thus more friction (Karroll, 2024).

Tire Pressure

Low tire pressure causes the tire walls to flex which pulls the tire tread to lift away from the road leaving less points of contact. Tire pressure must also stay low enough that the width of the tire stays on the road as excessive pressure causes the center of the tire tread to take most of the load (Karroll, 2024).

Too Much Friction

Although friction is crucial to the function of tires, if the force applied by the vehicle cannot overcome the static friction of the tires against the ground it will not move. Also, as tires move across the ground, they are damaged by constant friction. This is why we do not use snow tires for the entire year, as the extra friction causes extra damage to the tires and may cause difficulty with static friction in high friction driving environments.
